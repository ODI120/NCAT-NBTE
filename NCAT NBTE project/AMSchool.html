COURSE REQUIRMENT

Criteria for Admission

The academic requirements for admission into the HND Aviation management Program are:
i. Five (5) G.C.E.(0 Level), WASC, NECO or NABTEB subject passed at not more than two sitting. The subjects must be passed at credit level and should include English Language Mathematics and any two other subjects.
ii. A minimum of ND at not less than lower credit in Business Administration and Management, Maritime Transport and Business studies, marketing, Accountancy, Tourism, cooperative Economic and Management and Purchasing and Supply.
iii. One year relevant Industrial Work Experience. However in exceptional cases a candidate with a pass at ND Level must have acquired at least two year of cognate experience in order to qualify for admission.

Programme mission and Goals:

HNDAM aims to address the following themes in respect of aviation management and its allied discipline: .Develop the operational knowledge involved in airline business operation, management of aviation projects with commercial undertone, understand the dynamics of alternative transportation systems, and the management of fares and interline systems. .Grounded exposure and rudiment of aircraft operating cost and management of scarce revenue, holistic understanding of air transport economics, human factors.

Completion Criteria

For any student to qualify or the HNDAM, he/she must satisfy the following conditions:
a. Each student must complete althe Eighty (84) Credit Units (CU)
b. should attain minimum of 75% attendance.
c. Should effectively participate in the tutorial and submit a seminar paper where required.
d. should take the continuous assessment (course work, test and practical) which must be graded and form part of the assessment.
e. Should undertake a properly supervised and graded project.
f. Should take and pass the end of course examinations.

Grading

letter grade and numerical point sall be awarded to each student based on the total scores on all the evaluation criteria.The final marks scored by a student and the correspoonding letter grades and the numerical points will be clearly indicated. The grading system is done using the four-point grading and a student's performance in an recoorded as the Grade Point Average (GPA), which is a function of the Course Unit stand the Weighted Grade Point (WGP).
